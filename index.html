What I’m working on

ALICE Run 3 charged-particle jets
Main analyzer for the thesis paper, R-dependent charged-particle jet production in pp at √s = 13.6 TeV with ALICE (in preparation, 2026).
Tracking-efficiency systematics (HP 2026 prep)
Contributed the pp 13.6 TeV total tracking-efficiency systematic (~1.5%) for the PWG-JE Hard Probes 2026 preparation; ITS-TPC matching code reused by a PWG-JE colleague as a starting point for PbPb tracking studies.
ePIC Barrel Imaging Calorimeter
Lead offline analyst for the CERN PS T10 (2025.07) test-beam paper (in preparation, 2026); on-site DAQ shifter and co-author for the KEK AF–AR (2025.03) campaign; co-author on the first beam-test paper (submitted, NIMA-D-26-00482).
ALICE O2Physics framework
37 pull requests (18 merged) including a central-framework patch in Common/Tools (PR #15174, TrackTuner) and authored jetCrossSectionEfficiency.cxx (PR #14057), the PWG-JE task used by the SKKU Run 3 charged-jet analyses for cross-section normalization.
Mentoring and community
Co-mentor of two MSc co-analyzers on the ALICE Run 3 O–O charged-jet measurement; co-organizer and instructor of the KoALICE O2 Tutorial (SKKU, 2024.01).
