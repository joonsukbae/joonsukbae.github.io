Joonsuk Bae

PhD candidate, Department of Physics, Sungkyunkwan University. Experimental high-energy nuclear physics — ALICE Run 3 jets and ePIC barrel imaging calorimetry.

I work on charged-particle jet measurements in pp collisions with ALICE at the LHC, and on Pb–scintillator-fiber calorimeter R&D for the Electron–Ion Collider. I am interested in how non-perturbative QCD and initial-state effects shape jets in small systems.

What I’m working on

  • ALICE Run 3. Leading the first charged-particle jet cross-section measurement on Run 3 pp data at √s = 13.6 TeV, from detector-level QA to final unfolded spectra and the full systematics program.
  • ePIC BIC. Beam-test analysis and JANA2 reconstruction studies of the Pb/SciFi calorimeter prototype for the Electron–Ion Collider; sampling-fraction and resolution studies at CERN T10 and KEK AF–AR.
  • Jet substructure. Exploring observables sensitive to hadronization and quenching-like signatures in high-multiplicity pp and light-ion systems.
