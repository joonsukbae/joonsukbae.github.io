Research

I study how jets — collimated sprays of hadrons produced in high-energy collisions — encode the dynamics of QCD across scales, from the perturbative parton shower down to the soft, non-perturbative physics that governs hadronization. Most of my time is spent on the first Run 3 jet measurements at ALICE; in parallel I work on calorimeter R&D for the Electron–Ion Collider.

Programme 01

Jet physics in ALICE Run 3

I am the lead analyzer of the first charged-particle jet cross-section measurement on Run 3 pp collision data at √s = 13.6 TeV. The analysis spans the full chain: detector-level QA on continuously read-out data, jet reconstruction in O2Physics, response-matrix building, unfolding, and a complete systematic-uncertainty programme covering tracking effects and unfolding stability.

Beyond inclusive spectra, I am studying the R-dependence of the jet cross section and semi-inclusive recoil-jet distributions, which probe the interplay between perturbative radiation and underlying-event activity. My thesis develops these measurements into a test for quenching-like signatures in small systems.

Within the ALICE Jet Physics group I established the Run 3-specific cross-section normalization and efficiency strategy, accounting for the new continuous-readout event-selection definitions used since 2022.

Programme 02

Calorimetry for the Electron–Ion Collider

I work on the Barrel Imaging Calorimeter (BIC) for the ePIC detector at the EIC. On the hardware side, I have contributed to fabrication and assembly of Pb–scintillator-fiber modules with PMT and SiPM readout: module alignment, polishing of readout surfaces, and mechanical integration, followed by PMT–module QA with cosmic-ray and radioactive-source data, timing coincidences, and optical-coupling stability tests.

I have taken part in test-beam campaigns at CERN (T10–PS) and KEK (AF–AR), running on-site calibration, equalization, and prompt analysis. Offline I lead studies of energy response, resolution, and linearity, and Geant4 / JANA2 (EIC-recon) reconstruction work to determine energy- and η-dependent sampling fractions for the BIC.

Earlier

LAMPS forward tracker

From 2022 to 2024 I contributed to early-stage studies of an MPPC–scintillator-plane forward tracking detector for the LAMPS experiment at RAON: surveys of sensor options, performance specifications, and preliminary Geant4 simulations of detector geometry and tracking concepts.

Interests

What I am thinking about

  • Jet production and non-perturbative QCD: observables sensitive to hadronization and soft physics.
  • Jet substructure as a probe for quenching-like signatures in high-multiplicity pp and light-ion systems.
  • Initial-state effects and CGC-inspired frameworks for interpreting small-system jet measurements.
  • Detector R&D and performance characterization through test-beam data and simulation.
